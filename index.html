PREPRINT SUBMITTED · AFRICARXIV · MAY 2026
Seasonal Solar Irradiance Variability and Harmattan Effects Across West African Climate Zones: Evidence from NASA POWER Satellite Data Across 18 Cities (2014–2025)
Key finding: The Harmattan effect on solar irradiance is non-uniform across West Africa — enhancing GHI in Coastal Humid (+0.574 kWh/m²/day) and Savanna (+0.771 kWh/m²/day) zones while suppressing it in Desert/Arid zones (-1.059 kWh/m²/day). All differences confirmed statistically significant (Mann-Whitney U, p ≤ 0.009).

Target journal: Energy for Sustainable Development (Elsevier) · ORCID: 0009-0006-1174-9289
